Code & Theory EXT

The EXT Value Creation Framework

A structured, evidence-based system for measuring, communicating, and proving the business value generated by content supply chain transformation — from the first commercial conversation through the full lifespan of the engagement.

Enterprise content supply chain programmes — powered by Adobe Firefly Services, GenStudio, and Creative Cloud — deliver real, quantifiable business outcomes. Faster time to market, lower production costs, higher conversion rates, greater content reach. Yet too often this value goes unmeasured, understated, or absent from commercial conversations entirely.

EXT exists to change that. This framework gives every Engagement Manager a common language, a structured set of metrics, and a repeatable method for translating programme activity into business impact — one that speaks to the CFO and the CMO in equal measure. It operationalizes the promise of content supply chain transformation into the kind of confirmed, client-validated evidence that protects and grows accounts.

Metrics are organised into four discrete value buckets — drawn from the EXT Value Framework — that together provide a complete, non-overlapping view of programme performance. Each metric is tagged by its primary value driver, revenue growth or cost reduction, so the framework can be calibrated to the commercial priorities of any client, in any industry, at any stage of the engagement.

01
Value-Based Selling
Before the contract is signed, use the framework to anchor conversations on measurable business outcomes — not deliverables or hours. Shift the commercial model from time-and-materials to value-based pricing by identifying which metrics matter most to this client and establishing what a successful content supply chain programme looks like in dollar terms.
02
Rationalize — Engagement Kickoff
At the start of every engagement, agree a baseline for each selected metric with the client. Document the current state — cycle times, agency spend, asset volume, conversion rates — so future improvements are undeniable and grounded in the client's own data. This is the foundation of the RMO Rationalize phase: strategic clarity before deployment.
03
Modernize & Optimize — Active Delivery
Throughout the programme, track P1 metrics weekly and P2/P3 metrics monthly. Use the framework to surface adoption risks early, validate that content supply chain workflows are delivering at scale, and keep executive sponsors engaged with a clear, consistent picture of value realised — embedded within the Modernize and Optimize phases of the RMO methodology.
04
Renewal & Expansion
At every QBR and renewal conversation, the framework produces a ready-made, client-confirmed value story. Confirmed ROI figures, a growing innovation pipeline, and rising platform surface coverage are the three signals that make renewal and expansion conversations materially stronger — and move the relationship from execution partner to strategic platform.

01 Financial Impact
What has content supply chain transformation delivered in dollar terms?
Engagement Renewal ARR
Dollar savings — labor (hours saved × blended rate)
CostP1▼
Definition
Total hours saved multiplied by the fully loaded designer and marketer blended hourly rate. Must include both designer and marketer time to be accurate — excluding either understates the true labour saving.
How it's measured
Workfront task time logs (current FFCP workflow) vs. historical manual baseline. Finance ERP provides the blended rate. Compare time-per-asset before and after automation at workflow level.
Priority justification
P1 — the most universally understood CFO metric. Converts abstract time savings into "hard money" recoverable budget. Required for every engagement as the anchor for value-based commercial conversations.
Agency & production cost avoided ($)
CostP1▼
Definition
Total external spend reduction vs. pre-automation baseline: agency retainers, freelance production fees, stock asset licensing, and localisation vendor costs eliminated or reduced.
How it's measured
Finance and procurement records. Compare external production invoices in the measurement period against the equivalent period prior to automation. Requires a clean baseline established at engagement kickoff.
Priority justification
P1 — primary value driver for FSI clients (JPMC, Wells Fargo) where marketing is not a direct revenue lever. Agency cost is often the single largest discretionary spend line in a content budget and the most visible saving to a CFO.
Cost per asset (fully loaded production spend ÷ approved assets)
CostP1▼
Definition
Total production cost (fully loaded: labour, tooling, vendor, and overhead) divided by the number of approved assets produced in the same period. Tracks unit economics as automation scales.
How it's measured
Finance time-tracking systems + FFCP run logs to allocate cost at scale per workflow run. Divide total spend by approved asset count from DAM or GenStudio output records for the same period.
Priority justification
P1 — demonstrates the compounding value of automation: as volume grows, cost per unit falls. The downward cost curve is the clearest visual representation of programme ROI and works in every industry vertical.
FTE equivalent reclaimed (hours saved ÷ 1,900 hrs)
CostP2▼
Definition
Total hours saved in the measurement period divided by 1,900 (standard annual working hours). Converts abstract time savings into a headcount equivalent — e.g. "3.2 FTE-E reclaimed."
How it's measured
Derived from the dollar savings (labor) metric. HR data confirms the blended FTE cost. Calculated as: Total Hours Saved ÷ 1,900. Source: HR data + Workfront.
Priority justification
P2 — resonates strongly in resource reallocation conversations. Shifts the narrative from "we saved money" to "we freed up capacity for higher-value strategic work." Particularly compelling for technology and FSI clients managing headcount growth.
Incremental revenue attributed to content (content ROI / lift)
RevenueP2▼
Definition
(Incremental revenue attributable to automated content − production cost) ÷ production cost. Measures the net return on content investment, isolating the lift generated by automation-enabled assets over control creative.
How it's measured
A/B or multivariate testing: AI-generated variants vs. human control assets. Requires persistent content metadata to track an asset through to storefront conversion. Source: Web/app analytics + ad platforms + Adobe Customer Journey Analytics.
Priority justification
P2 — the most commercially compelling metric for revenue-weighted industries (Disney, NFL, Best Buy, Tesco). Requires test infrastructure to implement but produces the most credible renewal narrative when available.
Asset reuse rate (assets reused ÷ total available for reuse)
CostP3▼
Definition
Number of assets actively reused in the measurement period divided by total assets available for reuse in the DAM. A high reuse rate indicates a successful shift to a modular content strategy — e.g. one digital twin serving 100+ AI-generated scenes.
How it's measured
DAM / Content Hub usage logs + GenStudio asset tracking. Requires consistent asset taxonomy and tagging at ingestion. Compare asset creation events vs. asset retrieval/reuse events for the same content objects.
Priority justification
P3 — a maturity indicator for modular content programmes. Most relevant for CPG (product packshots), Grocery (catalog variations), and Retail (seasonal content). Requires a well-governed DAM before this metric becomes reliable.
Content-influenced pipeline / deal value
RevenueP3▼
Definition
Total value of deals, renewals, or upsells in which automated content played a documented, attributable role in the customer journey — from awareness through to commercial close.
How it's measured
CRM attribution data (Salesforce or equivalent) linked to content metadata. Requires the client to have multi-touch attribution in place. Best implemented as a quarterly retrospective analysis rather than a live metric.
Priority justification
P3 — highly compelling when available but depends on CRM attribution maturity. Most material for Media (Disney, NFL sponsorship/subscription) and Retail (Best Buy B2B and loyalty). Not feasible for FSI or most B2B clients without CRM investment.
02 Operational Excellence
How has the content supply chain physically changed?
Adoption Engagement
Time to market (brief approval → asset live in channel)
BothP1▼
Definition
Elapsed time in days/hours from brief approval to first asset live in its target distribution channel. Requires "last mile" tracking to confirm the asset is truly in-market — not just approved or uploaded.
How it's measured
Workfront timestamp (brief_approved) to activation platform timestamp (asset_live). Both endpoints must be captured to close the measurement loop. Source: Workfront + activation/channel platforms.
Priority justification
P1 — universal across all industries. Faster TTM has both a revenue dimension (earlier campaign capture, reacting to competitor moves in real time) and a cost dimension (fewer rush fees, overtime, and emergency vendor charges). Most intuitive metric for marketers and CMOs.
Content production rate (unique approved assets & variants per period)
CostP1▼
Definition
Total count of unique, approved assets and variants produced in the measurement period. Measures output capacity expansion without corresponding headcount growth — the primary signal that automation is delivering scale.
How it's measured
Workflow run outputs from FFCP / Firefly Services, especially for scaled variant production. Cross-referenced against DAM ingestion records for approved assets. Source: GenStudio / FFCP + DAM + Workfront.
Priority justification
P1 — the clearest operational proof that automation is working. Volume growth without headcount growth is a story every COO and CFO responds to. Directly answers "what did we actually build with this programme?"
Review cycle time (rounds & elapsed time from ready → approved)
CostP2▼
Definition
Average elapsed time and number of review rounds from "asset ready for review" to "approved and ready for distribution." Tracks whether speed gains in production are being absorbed by downstream review bottlenecks.
How it's measured
Workfront or Frame.io workflow timestamps. Variance tracking: monitor if AI-generated volume increases review load compared to manually produced assets. Source: Workfront / Frame.io.
Priority justification
P2 — critical governance metric. A programme that doubles production rate but also doubles review rounds delivers no net speed improvement. Particularly important for FSI and Healthcare where legal/brand is a known bottleneck.
Brand consistency score (AI assets passing review without rework)
CostP2▼
Definition
Percentage of AI-assisted assets that meet brand, legal, and compliance standards on first submission, without requiring rework. A score of 1–10 derived from QA sampling or automated brand intelligence scoring.
How it's measured
Periodic manual audits (QA sampling) or automated scoring via Brand Intelligence tools. Firefly Design Intelligence and Custom Models can be used to enforce guardrails and reduce first-pass failure rates. Source: Brand Intelligence + QA workflows.
Priority justification
P2 — especially material for FSI (JPMC, Wells Fargo) where off-brand or non-compliant assets carry regulatory exposure, and for Healthcare (pharma compliance). High first-pass rate = lower review overhead = faster time to market.
Content variant coverage (markets / SKUs / channels automated)
RevenueP2▼
Definition
Number of localised, channel-specific, or SKU-level content variants produced through automation that were not previously feasible at this volume through manual production. Measures the expansion of content reach enabled by automation.
How it's measured
PIM + DAM + campaign data. Compare variant count in the measurement period against baseline variant count prior to automation. Segment by market, channel, and product category. Source: PIM + DAM + campaign analytics.
Priority justification
P2 — primary scale metric for Retail (product pages at SKU level), CPG (retailer co-op content), and Grocery (seasonal catalog). Demonstrates that automation enables content strategies that were simply not feasible before.
Creator productivity (output volume per FTE)
CostP2▼
Definition
Total approved asset output volume divided by the number of FTEs involved in content production for the same period. Tracks the efficiency of the workforce relative to total output — independent of the dollar value of that output.
How it's measured
Output volume from Workfront / GenStudio divided by FTE headcount data from HR systems for the same period. Efficiency variance: measure output volume against internal labour hours over time to track the productivity curve. Source: Workfront / GenStudio + HR FTE data.
Priority justification
P2 — ROI on GenAI tool adoption at the individual creator level. Demonstrates that the programme isn't just changing what gets built — it's changing how efficiently the team builds it. Useful for conversations about team structure and capacity planning.
03 Activation & Adoption
Is the programme live and are teams realising value at scale?
Activation Adoption
Automation rate (% of assets generated via FFCP / Firefly workflows)
CostP1▼
Definition
Automation Rate = (# assets generated via FFCP or Firefly Services workflows in period P) ÷ (total # assets produced in period P). Tracks what proportion of the content supply chain has been automated.
How it's measured
FFCP / Firefly Services job and run logs + Workfront. Identify AI-origin assets via API run metadata. Compare against total approved asset count from DAM for the same period. Source: FFCP / Firefly logs + Workfront + DAM.
Priority justification
P1 — the foundational Activation metric. Without a meaningful automation rate, all downstream financial and operational metrics are undermined. Maturity benchmarking: identifies which departments are AI adopters and where resistance or gaps remain.
Use cases live in production (real output, not pilots)
BothP1▼
Definition
Count of use cases generating real, business-consumed output in production — explicitly excluding pilots, sandboxes, and proof-of-concept environments. A use case is live when it is generating output that the business is actively using.
How it's measured
Tracked at the account level in Airtable (or equivalent). Confirmed by EM weekly. A use case transitions from pilot to live only when it has generated output consumed by the business for at least one full reporting cycle.
Priority justification
P1 — the primary Activation signal used by EXT leadership to assess whether an account is on track. Directly answers "is the programme delivering?" in the simplest possible terms. Universal across all industries and engagement types.
Use case success metric hit rate (% meeting agreed KPIs)
BothP1▼
Definition
Percentage of live use cases that are meeting the success metrics agreed at activation. Expressed as a hit rate: 4 of 5 live use cases hitting targets = 80% hit rate. An account can be well-activated but poorly adopted — this metric surfaces that gap.
How it's measured
Tracked weekly per account. Success metrics are agreed at use case activation and documented in the engagement record. EM reviews each live use case against its target and records hit/miss status. Source: EM weekly update + use case KPI log.
Priority justification
P1 — separates activation from adoption. Having use cases live is necessary but not sufficient; they must also be performing. This metric is the primary early warning signal for engagement risk and the primary evidence of value delivered.
Active users / teams on platform (adoption breadth)
CostP2▼
Definition
Number of distinct users or teams actively generating or consuming automated content via platform workflows in the reporting period. Measures adoption breadth — the horizontal spread of the programme across the organisation.
How it's measured
Platform login and usage analytics from GenStudio, FFCP, or AEM. Define "active" as at least one workflow run or content action in the reporting period. Source: Platform usage logs.
Priority justification
P2 — low adoption breadth is the earliest leading indicator of shelfware risk. Complements automation rate (which tracks depth of automation) — a programme can have high automation rate in one team but zero spread across the rest of the organisation.
Workflow automation penetration (% of eligible workflows automated)
CostP2▼
Definition
Percentage of content workflows that have been automated versus the total number of eligible workflows mapped at engagement kickoff. Tracks penetration of automation across the addressable content supply chain.
How it's measured
Requires a workflow map created at engagement kickoff identifying all automatable content production processes. Track which workflows have been automated in each reporting period. Source: Engagement workflow map + FFCP run logs.
Priority justification
P2 — critical for demonstrating programme value vs. total addressable opportunity. A 30% penetration rate tells a very different story from 80%. Also surfaces the expansion opportunity for renewal conversations: the remaining 70% is the upsell pipeline.
Personalisation depth (data-enabled variants per master creative)
RevenueP2▼
Definition
Number of data-enabled content variants produced per master creative asset. Tracks the shift from broadcast to hyper-personalised content — moving from 1–2 manually produced variables to 5+ automated, audience-specific variants per master.
How it's measured
Cohort analysis: compare number of audience segments reached per campaign before and after automation. Source: AEP (Adobe Experience Platform) + AEM Assets. Track variant count per campaign relative to master asset count.
Priority justification
P2 — most material for consumer-facing industries where personalisation directly drives engagement and revenue: Disney, NFL (fan segments), Best Buy (product affinity), Tesco (loyalty segments). Note: distinct from personalisation scale in Bucket 4, which measures audience reach, not production depth.
Localisation & variant coverage (% of content localised across markets)
BothP2▼
Definition
Share of available reusable content that has been localised across all required markets, measured as a percentage of total market/locale coverage. Tracks completeness of content availability, not just volume of variants produced.
How it's measured
Market gap analysis: compare localised asset availability against regional market requirements defined at engagement kickoff. Source: PIM + DAM + campaign analytics. Segment by market and content type.
Priority justification
P2 — distinct from content variant coverage (B2, which counts volume) — this measures market completeness. Highest priority for Tesco (UK regional variations), 3M (global industrial markets), and any client operating across 10+ markets.
CSC maturity index (organisational AI & content supply chain score)
CostP3▼
Definition
A qualitative index scored 1–5 tracking the client organisation's overall maturity in AI adoption and content supply chain management. Covers process, technology, governance, people, and culture dimensions.
How it's measured
Periodic self-assessment against a standardised CSC maturity model via surveys and workshops facilitated by the EM. Benchmarking: compare the client's score against industry averages and against their own score at engagement kickoff. Source: CSC maturity surveys / workshops.
Priority justification
P3 — a strategic leading indicator rather than an operational metric. Vital for tracking the cultural and organisational shift required for GenAI success at scale. Most useful for long-term engagements where change management is a significant programme component.
04 Strategic & Platform Value
Is Adobe becoming central to how the client operates and grows?
Renewal ARR Activation
Innovation pipeline (validated future use cases committed by sponsor)
RevenueP1▼
Definition
Number of future use cases that the client's business sponsor has explicitly committed to building in the next programme phase. A use case enters the innovation pipeline only when it has been validated (scoped, prioritised, and sponsor-approved) — not merely discussed.
How it's measured
Tracked at the account level in the engagement record. Updated quarterly (or following each strategic review). EM confirms pipeline entries with the client's business sponsor in writing. Source: EM account record + sponsor sign-off.
Priority justification
P1 — the strongest leading indicator of expansion ARR. A growing pipeline signals that the client sees Adobe as a long-term platform partner, not a point solution provider. Critical for every renewal conversation regardless of industry.
Customer-confirmed value (sponsor sign-off on reported figures)
BothP1▼
Definition
Binary confirmation (yes/no) from the client's finance or marketing ops contact that the reported ROI figures are accurate and validated. Moves the value story from "we believe" to "the client agrees in writing." Without this, renewal conversations rest on unconfirmed estimates.
How it's measured
Formal sign-off obtained at each QBR. EM presents the cumulative value figure and requests written confirmation from a named client contact. Recorded in the engagement record with date and confirming contact name. Source: QBR record + client confirmation email or document.
Priority justification
P1 — the most important single metric for protecting renewal ARR. Confirmed value is categorically more persuasive than unconfirmed value in any commercial negotiation. Required at every QBR as a non-negotiable programme governance step.
Adobe surface coverage (distinct products actively in use)
BothP2▼
Definition
Number of distinct Adobe products actively in use by the client in the measurement period. Tracks platform centralisation — the degree to which Adobe has moved from a point solution to a core operating platform for the client's content function.
How it's measured
Tracked quarterly at the account level (surface coverage changes slowly). EM confirms active product usage with the client's technology or marketing ops lead. Source: EM account record + Adobe licence/usage data where available.
Priority justification
P2 — high surface coverage creates switching cost and signals deep platform integration. Each additional product in active use increases renewal likelihood and expansion potential. Tracked quarterly rather than weekly given its slow rate of change.
Cross-channel content reach (channels powered by automation)
RevenueP2▼
Definition
Number of distinct distribution channels (web, paid social, email, in-store, app, OTT, print) where automated content is actively published and consumed. Measures how broadly Adobe powers the client's content go-to-market motion.
How it's measured
Channel activation records cross-referenced against automated content workflow outputs. A channel counts as active when it has received automation-produced content in the reporting period. Source: Campaign analytics + DAM distribution records.
Priority justification
P2 — breadth of channel coverage signals strategic centrality. A client using automated content across 8 channels is far more dependent on the programme than one using it in 2. High channel coverage is a strong renewal and expansion signal for consumer-facing clients.
Content personalisation scale (distinct audience segments served)
RevenueP2▼
Definition
Number of distinct audience segments actively receiving personalised, automation-produced content in the measurement period. Measures the strategic reach of personalisation — how broadly the client is deploying the 1:1 content capability that automation enables. Distinct from personalisation depth (B3), which measures variants per master creative.
How it's measured
AEP segment data cross-referenced with campaign personalisation records. Source: Adobe Experience Platform (AEP) + campaign analytics.
Priority justification
P2 — personalisation at scale is the competitive differentiation Adobe enables that most clients could not achieve manually. Most material for Disney (fan segments), NFL (fan affinity), Best Buy (product affinity), and Tesco (loyalty tiers).
Regulatory & brand risk incident reduction
CostP2▼
Definition
Reduction in the number of compliance incidents, legal holds, brand safety violations, or regulatory findings attributable to content, compared against the pre-automation baseline. Tracks risk reduction as a measurable business value.
How it's measured
Legal and compliance records from the client's risk management function. Compare incident count in the measurement period against the equivalent period prior to automation. Requires a clean baseline and consistent incident classification. Source: Client legal/compliance records.
Priority justification
P2 — highest strategic value for FSI (JPMC, Wells Fargo regulatory exposure) and Healthcare (pharma promotional compliance). Difficult to quantify as a dollar figure but extremely compelling in board-level conversations about the true cost of non-compliance.
Executive sponsor engagement & advocacy
BothP2▼
Definition
Presence and active engagement of a C-level or VP-level sponsor in programme reviews, QBRs, and strategic planning sessions. Serves as a proxy for the strategic importance of the programme to the account. Tracked as a qualitative signal, not a numeric metric.
How it's measured
EM tracks sponsor attendance at each programme governance touchpoint. Qualitative notes on sponsor sentiment and advocacy captured in the account record. Low executive engagement triggers an account risk flag. Source: EM account notes + meeting records.
Priority justification
P2 — the leading risk indicator for renewal. Programmes that lose executive sponsorship rarely survive the renewal cycle regardless of their operational metrics. Monitoring and maintaining senior engagement is one of the highest-ROI activities an EM can invest in.

MECE design principle. Each metric measures exactly one distinct dimension of value and maps to exactly one bucket. Bucket 1 captures dollar outcomes from content supply chain investment. Bucket 2 captures operational throughput and process efficiency within the content supply chain. Bucket 3 captures programme activation and adoption depth across teams and workflows. Bucket 4 captures strategic positioning and platform entrenchment — the degree to which Adobe is becoming central to how the client operates. No metric appears in more than one bucket. Where two metrics could appear adjacent — e.g. personalisation depth (B3: variants per master creative, a production metric) vs. personalisation scale (B4: audience segments reached, a market coverage metric) — they are retained as distinct because they measure genuinely different things.
