EXT Value Creation Framework • Overview • Framework • Industries CODE & THEORY EXT The EXT Value Creation Framework A structured, evidence-based system for measuring, communicating, and proving the business value generated by content supply chain transformation — from the first commercial conversation through the full lifespan of the engagement. Enterprise content supply chain programmes — powered by Adobe Firefly Services, GenStudio, and Creative Cloud — deliver real, quantifiable business outcomes. Faster time to market, lower production costs, higher conversion rates, greater content reach. Yet too often this value goes unmeasured, understated, or absent from commercial conversations entirely. EXT exists to change that. This framework gives every Engagement Manager a common language, a structured set of metrics, and a repeatable method for translating programme activity into business impact — one that speaks to the CFO and the CMO in equal measure. It operationalizes the promise of content supply chain transformation into the kind of confirmed, client-validated evidence that protects and grows accounts. Metrics are organised into four discrete value buckets — drawn from the EXT Value Framework — that together provide a complete, non-overlapping view of programme performance. Each metric is tagged by its primary value driver, revenue growth or cost reduction, so the framework can be calibrated to the commercial priorities of any client, in any industry, at any stage of the engagement. 01 Value-Based Selling Before the contract is signed, use the framework to anchor conversations on measurable business outcomes — not deliverables or hours. Shift the commercial model from time-and-materials to value-based pricing by identifying which metrics matter most to this client and establishing what a successful content supply chain programme looks like in dollar terms. 02 Rationalize — Engagement Kickoff At the start of every engagement, agree a baseline for each selected metric with the client. Document the current state — cycle times, agency spend, asset volume, conversion rates — so future improvements are undeniable and grounded in the client's own data. This is the foundation of the RMO Rationalize phase: strategic clarity before deployment. 03 Modernize & Optimize — Active Delivery Throughout the programme, track P1 metrics weekly and P2/P3 metrics monthly. Use the framework to surface adoption risks early, validate that content supply chain workflows are delivering at scale, and keep executive sponsors engaged with a clear, consistent picture of value realised — embedded within the Modernize and Optimize phases of the RMO methodology. 04 Renewal & Expansion At every QBR and renewal conversation, the framework produces a ready-made, client-confirmed value story. Confirmed ROI figures, a growing innovation pipeline, and rising platform surface coverage are the three signals that make renewal and expansion conversations materially stronger — and move the relationship from execution partner to strategic platform. Each metric measures exactly one distinct dimension of value and maps to exactly one bucket. Bucket 1 captures dollar outcomes from content supply chain investment. Bucket 2 captures operational throughput and process efficiency within the content supply chain. Bucket 3 captures programme activation and adoption depth across teams and workflows. Bucket 4 captures strategic positioning and platform entrenchment — the degree to which Adobe is becoming central to how the client operates. No metric appears in more than one bucket. Where two metrics could appear adjacent — e.g. personalisation depth (B3: variants per master creative, a production metric) vs. personalisation scale (B4: audience segments reached, a market coverage metric) — they are retained as distinct because they measure genuinely different things.
